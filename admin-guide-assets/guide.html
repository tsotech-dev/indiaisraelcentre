Introduction

The India Israel Centre website is powered by Payload CMS, a headless content management system. All site content — publications, people profiles, convenings, and static page copy — is managed through the admin panel.

This guide walks through every section of the admin panel with screenshots. You do not need any technical knowledge to use it — if you can fill in a form, you can manage the site.

Admin URL https://indiaisraelcentre.org/admin
Local development URL http://localhost:3000/admin
Note: Always use the live URL (indiaisraelcentre.org/admin) for real content. The localhost URL is only for development and testing on your own machine.
01

Logging In

Navigate to the admin URL and enter your email and password. Each team member should have their own login — do not share credentials.

02

Dashboard & Navigation

After logging in you land on the dashboard. The left sidebar lists every section of the admin:

Collections (dynamic content with multiple entries): Users, Media, Publications, People, Convenings, Newsletter Subscribers, Pillars.

Page Content / Globals (single-entry pages): Homepage, About, Forum, Research, Privacy, Terms, and more.

03

Publications

Publications are the Centre's research output. There are three types: Papers, Briefs, and Commentary. Each publication appears on the relevant research page on the site.

Click Publications in the left sidebar to see all entries.

03

Creating a Publication

Click Create New (top right) to add a publication. Fill in each field:

FieldRequired?Notes
TitleRequiredFull title of the publication. Used in the page heading and browser tab.
SlugRequiredURL-friendly ID, e.g. india-israel-water-tech. Only lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens. Must be unique.
TypeRequiredPaper, Brief, or Commentary. Determines which listing page it appears on.
PillarRequiredSelect the research pillar this publication belongs to.
AuthorsRequired (min 1)Add one or more authors. Each has a Name and optional Affiliation.
Published DateRequiredThe publication date — appears on cards and the article page.
AbstractOptional1–3 sentence summary shown on listing cards and previews.
BodyOptionalFull article text with rich formatting (headings, bold, links, lists). Paste or type here.
PDF (Supporting files)OptionalUpload a PDF version. Expand the "Supporting files" section at the bottom.
DOIOptionalDigital Object Identifier string if available.
Slug tip: Make it short and descriptive. The URL will be /research/papers/your-slug/. Once published and shared, avoid changing the slug.

People

The People collection manages researcher and team profiles that appear on the About → People page.

Each person has a name, role, short bio (rich text), and an optional photo.

05

Convenings

Convenings are the Centre's events — roundtables, public lectures, and dialogues. They appear on the Forum page.

Upcoming convenings (future event dates) are shown prominently. Past convenings move to the archive.

05

Convenings Fields Reference

FieldRequired?Notes
TitleRequiredName of the event.
SlugRequiredURL: /forum/slug/. Lowercase, hyphens.
FormatOptionalRoundtable, Public Lecture, or Dialogue.
Event DateRequiredDate of the event. Upcoming events (future dates) are highlighted on the forum page.
LocationOptionalPhysical venue or "Online".
DescriptionOptionalRich text description of the event, agenda, and participants.
Cover ImageOptionalUpload an event photo or banner.
Past vs upcoming: The site automatically shows convenings with a future Event Date as "upcoming" and past ones in the archive. Simply set the correct date — no toggle needed.
06

Media Library

The Media collection is a central library for all uploaded files — images and PDFs. Files uploaded here can be reused across multiple publications and pages.

You can upload directly via a publication or person form, or come here first to upload and organise files.

06

Uploading Media

1
Click Media in the left sidebar.
2
Click Create New in the top right.
3
Drag and drop a file, or click the upload area to browse. Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, PDF.
4
Add an Alt text for images — this is important for accessibility and SEO.
5
Click Save. The file is now available to attach to any publication, person, or convening.
Image sizing: For publication covers and people photos, use images at least 800px wide. For PDFs, any size is fine.

Newsletter Subscribers

This collection stores everyone who has signed up for the Centre's newsletter via the website footer or homepage form.

This is a read-only list for admin reference. Subscribers are added automatically when someone signs up on the site.

08

Pillars

The Pillars collection defines the six research pillars of the Centre. These appear in the research navigation and on publication cards.

The six pillars are pre-configured. Only edit them if the Centre formally redefines its research areas.

09

Page Content — Globals

Globals are single-entry records that control the copy on specific pages. Unlike collections (which have many entries), each global has exactly one version.

Use globals to update page headings, introductory text, and featured descriptions without a developer.

Tips & Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to keep content consistent and the site running smoothly.

Slugs

Keep slugs short and permanent. The slug becomes the URL. Once a publication is live and shared or indexed by Google, changing the slug breaks that link. Choose carefully before publishing.

Good: bene-israel-diaspora-identity
Avoid: publication-1 or draft-testing-123

Publication Body — Rich Text

Pasting from Word or Google Docs: Use Paste as plain text (Ctrl+Shift+V / Cmd+Shift+V) to avoid carrying over invisible formatting. Then apply headings and bold within the editor.

Use the toolbar to add Heading 2 and Heading 3 for section titles, Bold for key terms, and the link icon for hyperlinks.

Images

Image formats: JPEG for photos, PNG for logos with transparency, WebP for optimised web images.

Alt text: Always fill in the alt text when uploading to the Media library. It describes the image for screen readers and search engines. Example: "Professor Khinvraj Jangid at the India-Israel conference, 2024".

Saving

Always click Save. The admin does not auto-save. Changes are lost if you navigate away without saving. The Save button is at the top right of every form.

Publishing Workflow

1
Draft — Create the entry and fill in all fields. Save frequently.
2
Review — Share the admin link with a colleague for a proof-read (they need an admin account).
3
Publish — Once approved, save the final version. The entry goes live immediately on the public site.
4
Announce — Share the published URL on the Centre's social channels.

Getting Help

If you encounter an issue with the admin panel or content is not displaying correctly on the site, contact the technical team at research@indiaisraelcentre.org. Include a screenshot and a description of what you were trying to do.
